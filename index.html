About My Research

Computational Chemistry Molecular Dynamics Enhanced Sampling Machine Learning

I am a research scholar specializing in computational chemistry and atomistic simulations, applying theoretical and computational methods to solve complex chemical problems. My research combines quantum mechanics, molecular dynamics, and machine learning approaches to investigate molecular interactions, predict material properties, and simulate chemical reactions with atomic-level precision.

Through high-performance computing and advanced simulation techniques, my work bridges the gap between theoretical models and experimental observations, providing molecular-level insights that are challenging to obtain through experimental methods alone. I am passionate about developing novel computational methodologies that enhance our understanding of chemical phenomena and accelerate the discovery of new materials and compounds.
